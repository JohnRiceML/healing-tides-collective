# Reference Sites

Founder-provided references and what we should take (or leave) from each.

## Lifetime — coral-gables location
https://www.lifetime.life/locations/fl/coral-gables.html

**Take:**
- Confident, calm marketing voice — no over-promising
- Cinematic hero treatment with restraint (not maximalist)
- Clean typographic hierarchy
- Spaciousness — sections breathe

**Leave:**
- Corporate gym feel
- Stock-y people-in-pose photography
- Membership-tier-driven CTAs (we're not a membership)

## Kairos Collective
https://www.kairos-collective.com/

**Take:**
- Editorial, considered tone
- "Collective" as positioning — aligned with our name
- Subtle motion and reveal patterns
- Practitioner-as-craft framing

**Leave:**
- If anything reads as too niche/insider, soften it for a broader audience

## prAna (San Diego Wellness Week creative — image ref `02-prana-wellness-week.png`)
Brand reference, not a website to copy structurally.

**Take:**
- Bold, confident display type over cinematic photography
- Real bodies in real practice (warrior pose) — not stock posing
- Restraint in color — muted, cinematic, almost monochromatic
- Headlines that take up real estate without apology

**Leave:**
- Athletic / outdoor activewear edge — we're warmer and more editorial
- Their letterforms specifically — we want a serif, they use a condensed sans

## Sacred Woman Collective
https://www.sacredwomancollective.com/

**Take:**
- Warmth and intimacy in the visual language
- Soft photography direction
- Membership/community energy without feeling cliquey

**Leave:**
- Gendered framing (we're for everyone)
- Anything that reads as too spiritual — we sit between clinical and holistic

---

## Synthesizing the references

If our brand were a Venn diagram of these three, the overlap is:
- **Quiet confidence** (all three)
- **Editorial restraint** (Kairos especially)
- **Warmth without softness-into-fluff** (Sacred Woman)
- **Considered craft** (all three)

The center we're aiming for: *Lifetime's confidence + Kairos's editorial sensibility + Sacred Woman's warmth, minus any of their genre conventions.*
