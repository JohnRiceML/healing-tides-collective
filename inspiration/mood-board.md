## Founder-supplied references (2026-04-26)

### 01 — Poolside indoor/outdoor lounge (`01-poolside-lounge.png`)
A modern double-height wellness space. Wood plank floors, low platform daybeds with neutral cushions, indoor pool, mature plants (palms, birds of paradise, monstera), full-height glass to a courtyard.

**Read:** spaciousness, indoor-outdoor flow, "designed but lived-in," tropical greenery as a co-designer of the room. *Restorative without feeling like a spa cliché.*

**Take:** the *pace* of the image. Lots of negative space. Plants as architecture. Wood + glass + soft textile. Nothing precious.

### 02 — prAna San Diego Wellness Week (`02-prana-wellness-week.png`)
Three people in a yoga warrior pose against a wall of frosted/screened glass. Big confident wordmark + cinematic display headline ("SAN DIEGO WELLNESS WEEK") laid over the photo. Greenish-grey muted palette.

**Read:** *real bodies in real practice* — diverse, mid-motion, not posed-for-camera. Bold typographic confidence sitting on top of cinematic photography. The headline isn't shy.

**Take:** the courage to use **large, confident display type** over photography. The use of real practice (warrior pose) instead of stock wellness clichés. The muted, almost-cinematic color grade.

**Adjustment for our brand:** their type is more athletic / outdoor. Ours should feel a touch warmer and more editorial — same scale and confidence, slightly softer letterforms.

### 03 — Attic workspace (`03-attic-workspace.png`)
A pitched-ceiling room with white walls, a single person at a built-in desk under cork pinboards, leather mid-century chair, woven rug, fiddle leaf fig, soft natural light from a dormer.

**Read:** *thoughtful, considered, a little eclectic.* This is "a person works here" not "a brand performs here." Curated but not styled-to-death. A studio, not a showroom.

**Take:** the tactile mix — leather + wood + textile + paper + plants. The single-person scale. **This is the energy a practitioner profile or "about" page should carry.** Real work happens here.

### 04 — Spa courtyard with teak loungers (`04-spa-courtyard.png`)
Outdoor-feeling wellness courtyard. Teak loungers with white cushions and *pale-blue* accent pillows, mosaic tile feature wall (iridescent), small trees, ceramic vase, vaulted white ceiling with ambient sconces.

**Read:** *quiet luxury with a single color note.* The whole frame is neutral except the careful pop of teal/blue in the pillows and the mosaic. That restraint is the brand.

**Take:** **how to use color.** Most of the canvas is neutral; one or two surfaces carry the brand color with intention. Confirms our `--ocean` / `--teal` / `--seafoam` accent strategy from `docs/design-spec.md`.

## Synthesized direction (what the four images agree on)

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Space** | Generous. Air around everything. |
| **Materials** | Wood (warm, mid-tone), linen, ceramic, teak, iron/black hardware. |
| **Greenery** | Mature plants are non-negotiable — they're set dressing AND architecture. Fiddle leaf, monstera, palms, small trees. |
| **People** | Real, mid-action, not posed. One or two at a time. Practitioners working. Clients arriving. |
| **Color** | 90% neutral canvas. 10% intentional brand color (pale blues, sage, teal). |
| **Type** | Confident display, editorial restraint elsewhere. Big headlines earn their size. |
| **Light** | Soft, natural, often slightly cool but warm-balanced. Never harsh. |
| **Lifestyle** | Studio energy, not spa-marketing energy. Lived-in, considered. |

## What it should feel like to a first-time visitor
*"Oh — this is calmer than I expected. I can think here."*

## What it should never feel like
- A medical portal
- A wellness app gamified with streaks and badges
- A spa marketing brochure
- A tarot / astrology aesthetic
- A SaaS with abstract illustrations

## Photography prompts (when we go to source)
- A practitioner pouring tea, hands only, warm afternoon light, linen apron
- Back-of-shoulder view of someone in a quiet treatment room
- Close-up: journal, pen, water glass, no faces, soft window light
- A tide rolling in slowly on smooth sand, no people, no sky
- Wildflowers in a low ceramic vase on a wood surface
- Real practice: yoga, acupuncture setup, a movement therapist mid-cue (per the prAna ref — real bodies, real action)
- A studio interior with a fiddle leaf fig, leather chair, warm wood floors (per attic ref)
- A quiet spa courtyard with a single muted-teal cushion against neutral linen (per spa ref)

## Photography to never use
- Lavender on white sheets
- Women in white robes laughing
- Hands forming a heart
- Sunset yoga poses on a cliff
- Stock "happy diverse group looking at laptop"

## Type direction (refined after image review)
- Display headlines deserve to be **large and confident** (per prAna ref). The brand is calm but not timid.
- Display face: editorial serif with warmth and structure — *Fraunces* or *GT Sectra* preferred.
- For one-or-two-word hero statements, oversize. Let the type be the photography when there is no photography.
