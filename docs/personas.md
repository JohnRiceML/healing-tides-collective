# Personas

Three primary users. The platform serves all three with **one flow** and **adaptive language**, not three branches.

## Quick comparison

| | Overwhelmed Optimizer | Practical Seeker | Curious Seeker |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generation | Millennial / midlife | Gen X | Gen Z |
| Age | 35–50 | 45–60 | 18–29 |
| Life stage | Career + kids, very busy | Established, multi-role | Early adulthood / career |
| Mindset | Open but overwhelmed | Skeptical, practical | Curious, emotionally aware |
| Wellness exposure | Interested, not deeply informed | Late adopter, cautious | Highly exposed, trend-aware |
| Core problem | Too many choices | Doesn't trust options | Knows too much, can't decide |
| Emotional state | Mentally fatigued | Guarded, efficient | Anxious, overloaded |
| Internal voice | "I need help but don't know what to choose" | "I just want something that works" | "How do I know what's right for me?" |

## Strategic synthesis

All three want the same thing: **clarity + confidence in choosing care.** They get there differently.

Our platform plays three roles:
1. **A guide** — for everyone
2. **A filter** — especially for Millennial + Gen X
3. **A translator** — especially for Gen Z (turn feelings into next steps)

## Design implications

- **One entry point:** "Get Matched" (works for all three)
- **Adaptive tone in copy** — but no persona-specific UX branching
- **Short, guided flow** — no endless browsing
- **Visible reassurance** at every step (you can come back, no commitment, etc.)
- **No directory wall** — never show >3 options at once without context
